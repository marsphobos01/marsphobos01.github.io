Case study

Bespoke Water Solutions.

A responsive business website for a UK water-pump supplier, built end-to-end with a product catalogue and enquiry flow.

Project summary

Bespoke Water Solutions needed a clear, responsive web presence that explained its product categories and gave customers a direct way to enquire. I designed and built the site from initial layout through to production deployment.

Problem being solved

The business needed to present specialist water-pump products in a way that was easy to scan and credible on mobile and desktop. The site also needed a low-friction enquiry route so customers could ask about the right product category.

Target users

The users are commercial and domestic customers looking for water-pump equipment, often comparing suppliers and wanting fast contact rather than a complex checkout flow.

Architecture overview

The site is built with HTML, CSS and JavaScript, with a PHP-backed enquiry form. The content is organized around six product categories, with responsive layouts and direct calls to action across the page.

Important technical decisions

I kept the build framework-free so the site stayed lightweight, maintainable and easy to host. The product catalogue is structured for scanning first, because the main conversion path is an enquiry rather than an online purchase.

Difficult problems and solutions

The main challenge was balancing enough product information with a clean small-business site. The answer was to keep category pages and calls to action simple, then use the enquiry form to capture details that vary by customer need.

Results and lessons learned

The site is live in production. It reinforced the importance of matching technical choices to the client: a fast, simple and reliable site was more valuable here than a heavier CMS or frontend stack.
